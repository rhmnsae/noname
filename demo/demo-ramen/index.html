The Process

No Shortcuts.
Only Craft.

Our kitchen never sleeps. Every bowl you receive is the result of quiet obsession — a philosophy inherited directly from Hakata's ramen artisans and refined over years of study in Tokyo.

We source pork bones and chicken carcasses from certified farms, and our tare is fermented in-house with proprietary techniques that took two years to perfect.

01

Broth Begins at Midnight

Bones are blanched, cleaned, then placed in our heavy stock pots. The fire starts at 12 AM every night.

02

Noodles Made at Dawn

Our noodle chef arrives at 5 AM. Each batch is kneaded, rested, and cut by hand — adjusted daily for humidity.

03

Chashu Braised for Hours

Pork belly is marinated overnight, then slow-braised in mirin, sake, and soy for four hours until it melts.

04

The Bowl is Composed

Tare first, then hot broth, then noodles — every topping placed with intention. We time each bowl to the second.
